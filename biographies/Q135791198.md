# Norman Lee Bush
## Attended Yale Divinity School 1929-1930

Norman Lee Bush was born on January 20, 1900, in Aiken, South Carolina to Jacob Bush, a farmer, and Estell Bush. He attended South Carolina State University in Orangeburg where he was a member of the varsity debate team. He graduated from the institution in 1928 and enrolled at Yale Divinity School in the same year. He attended Yale Divinity School from 1928 to 1929 but left without a degree.[^1] 

He served as principal of Batesburg High School in Batesburg, South Carolina from 1934 to 1944. He also served as a dean of Bettis Academy and Junior College. In 1944, he spoke at South Carolina State University’s Founder’s Day celebration. In 1947, he transitioned careers and became a pastor of Friendship Baptist Church in Aiken. He would spend most of his life pastoring the church. In the 1960s, Bush worked with other pastors to organize on behalf of Black workers in Aiken. In 1965, Bush ran in the Democratic primary for Aiken City Council. He was elected as vice president of the city’s Democratic Club.[^2] 

In 1970, he officiated the wedding of Josephus Coan, another early Black Yale graduate from South Carolina. Bush died on October 31, 1981 in Evanston, Illinois and was buried in Aiken. The Friendship Baptist Church renamed their activity building in his honor as N. L. Bush Activity Building.[^3]
