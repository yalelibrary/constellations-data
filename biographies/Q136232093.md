# Walter Emory Hatfield 
## Attended Yale School of Medicine 1892-1893, 1894-1895 

Walter Emory Hatfield was born in 1869 to Jacob Hatfield and Mary J. Hatfield. According to the 1880 U.S. Census, his father, Jacob, was a workman at a rubber works and his mother, Mary, was a laundress. He enrolled at Yale School of Medicine for the 1892-1893 school year but left shortly thereafter. He re-enrolled at the school as a special student for the 1894-1895 school year. The 1900 U.S. Census reported that Hatfield lived in Boston, Massachusetts and worked as a cook. The 1910 U.S. Census reported that he worked as a cook in New Haven and lived on Goffe Street with his mother, wife Lillian Lansing Hatfield, and daughter Margaret. The 1920 U.S. Census reported that Hatfield lived on Gregory Street and worked at the Winchester Repeating Arms Company. He eventually moved to Brooklyn, New York where the 1930 U.S. Census reported that he was a clerk for a bank. In 1937, Hatfield’s wife, Lillian, died. Two years later, on November 11, 1939, Hatfield married Nellie Howell at St. Phillip’s Episcopal Church in Brooklyn. The 1950 U.S. Census reported that Hatfield was 80 years old and lived with Nellie in Brooklyn. He died in October 1968.[^1]
