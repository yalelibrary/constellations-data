# Van Sylvester Whitted
## Attended Yale School of Music 1916-1917
Van Sylvester Whitted was born in 1894 in Philadelphia. He was the son of Reverend Calvin S. Whitted, an AME Zion church official and the former pastor of New Haven's Varick AME Zion Church, and Reba Whitted. 

An accomplished musician, he attended the Yale School of Music from 1916-1917. When he registered for the draft in 1917, he listed his occupation as "janitor and student in Yale University" and reported that he lived at 276 Division Street in New Haven. 

He went on to graduate from Howard University with a degree in music in 1922, and continued his studies as an organist under the tutelage of T. Tertius Nobel. In 1926 he married Cora Louise Redmond.
