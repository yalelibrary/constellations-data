# Homer Thomas
## Received master's degree, 1912

Homer Thomas was born on February 5, 1879, to David Thomas and Eliza Rucker in Fincastle, Virginia. According to the 1880 census, the Thomas family were servants to another family. He graduated from the Hampton Normal and Agricultural Institute in Hampton, Virginia in 1902. He later enrolled at Oberlin College in Oberlin, Ohio, graduating with a bachelor’s degree in 1911. Later in the year, he enrolled at Yale Graduate School and received a master’s in education in 1912. Following his graduation, he began teaching at Florida Agricultural and Mechanical College in Tallahassee, Florida. He would go on to serve as a dean at Lincoln University, Bethune-Cookman College in Daytona Beach, Florida and Edward Waters in Jacksonville, Florida. He began teaching at Albany State College in 1943, where he was a professor of mathematics before retiring in 1954.[^1]

He died on September 28, 1959, and was buried in Gravel Hill Cemetery in Troutville, Virginia.[^2]
