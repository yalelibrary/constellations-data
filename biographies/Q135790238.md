# Frances Dingwall Corey
## Graduate of Yale School of Music, 1934

Frances White Dingwall was born on May 30, 1908, in New Haven, Connecticut to Joseph Nathaniel Dingwall and Sarah Elizabeth Bradley. Her father, Nathaniel, was a Jamaican immigrant. On her mother's side, she was a grandniece of Robert and Julia Pennington Gibbs Muse and first cousin once removed to two fellow Black graduates of Yale School of Music: Victoria Josephine Muse and Jessie Estell Muse. According to the 1910 US Census, the Dingwall family lived on 169 Bassett Street with the Muse family.[^1]  

Frances won a musical technique contest at The Nutmeg State Federation of Colored Women’s Clubs Convention in 1922. Mary McLeod Bethune, president of the National Association of Colored Women, appointed Frances as state chairman for music in 1928. She began attending the Yale School of Music in 1924 and periodically attended the school throughout the late 1920s and early 1930s. The *Yale Daily News* reported Frances’ participation at several recitals given at Dwight Memorial Chapel and Sprague Memorial Hall. She received a bachelor of music in 1934 from the school. According to the 1940 US Census, she worked as a piano teacher and was married to Norman Winifred Corey. Alongside her teaching role, Frances oversaw the Dixwell Glee Club. In the 1950 Census, Frances is listed as separated, farming, and living with her young daughters Madeline and Myra in Bethany, Connecticut. She died on August 10, 1951, and is buried in Evergreen Cemetery.[^2]
