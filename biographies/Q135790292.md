# John Warren Alexander
## Received Certificate of Proficiency in the Theory of Music, 1928

On May 14, 1905, John Warren Alexander (also known as Warren Alexander) was born to John G. Alexander, a hostler, and Hattie Williams Harrison, a seamstress in Boston, Massachusetts. He eventually moved to Seymour, Connecticut, where he attended Seymour High School, graduating in 1924. Warren Alexander served as an organist for the A.M.E. Zion Church where he played a piano solo for an Alpha Phi Alpha “Go to High School—Go to College” event. He enrolled at the Yale School of Music in 1924 and received a Certificate of Proficiency in the Theory of Music in 1928. He eventually began working at the American Bass Company in Ansonia and was married to Nettie Elizabeth. He moved to Boston and was married to Matilda Louise Marshall, according to the 1950 census. He died in 1983 and is buried in Blue Hill Cemetery in Braintree, Massachusetts.[^1]
